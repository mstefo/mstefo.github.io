Overwatch Workshop

What Is Overwatch Workshop?

Overwatch (2016) is a team based hero shooter developed by Activision Blizzard. In 2019, the game received an update to introduce the Workshop. This is a simplified scripting system using a proprietary language to expose the built-in systems of the game to any player. This webpage is dedicated to a portion of the Workshop projects I had created over the years.

Partner Puzzles

Partner Puzzles is a 2-player cooperative puzzle experience where the players must work together to figure out how to reach the exit. Unfortunately, due to the evolving nature of Overwatch as a live-service game, most of these projects have broken challenges.

Hanamura Havoc

Hanamura Havoc is a player versus environment (PVE) game mode where players attempt to fend off the endless hordes of custom AI-controlled heroes. Between rounds, players can unlock new abilities (called "Skills") to enhance their capabilities in order to survive as long as possible. There are over 150 Skills to unlock. In addition, every couple of rounds players will need to complete a certain objective in order to receive bonus rewards or, if they fail, strengthen the enemies. Finally, some rounds will have global effects added to disrupt players further such as forcing them to run forward or enemies exploding when eliminated

Game Designer

  • Designed over 150 unique purchasable skills

Colour Dance

Colour Dance was the first officially public Workshop Project that I had released. It is a Free-For-All (FFA) survival mode where players must reach the correctly coloured sphere before the time runs out. Players can use their chosen hero kits to move to the sphere more quickly or disrupt other players in order to be the last one standing & win.
